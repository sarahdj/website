Project Background

  • Client? Fork in the Road
  • Product/Service? Dine in food service, customer service.
  • SWOT: SWOT Analysis
  • Existing Research: No, we are using the current website and personal experience in the establishment to do this project.

Overview

  • What is the project? Redesign Fork in the Road Website
  • What are we designing and why? Updating the website to improve aesthetic appeal and functionality a
  • What opportunities are we trying to take advantage of? Popularity of small businesses and quirky instagram-worthy locations. Desire for comfort food in the midst of the pandemic .

Drivers

  • What is our goal for this project? To design this client’s website in a way that increases customer interaction with the business. The customer will be able to easily navigate this clients web page and have more avenues to engage with the business.
  • What are our top three objectives?
    1. Emphasize the charm of the restaurant and help them to create a more appealing website that is more indicative of their aesthetic.
    2. Attract customers seeking a distraction during the pandemic.
    3. Improve useability and organization of the site.
  • How long would the project take? We will work on and improve this project for as long as we can in order to ensure that we are delivering the best product possible. It is safe to assume that we will take up until the deadline.
  • How long until the client should expect to meet the above mentioned goals? About 5 weeks

Audience

  • Who’s our target audience?:
      Both locals and people visiting. Predominantly young adults (college students), recent grads, and families. The audience will be people of all ages. Due to the restaurant’s location (proximity to a University), the largest population of our audience will be ages 18-23, this age is typically more low income. This restaurant will be appealing to young, college graduate aged adults. Ages 23-30. Another audience will be families, including grandparents, so specifically parents and grandparents, making ages 30-80. The pricing is appealing to low income as well as others. Education level is likely low to middle, as a high amount of the customers will be students. With a majority of our target audience being college educated and young to middle aged, the likelihood that the majority of our population has a sufficient amount of computer knowledge is high. This includes a proclivity for engaging and using social media.
  • What do they think of us? That we are a welcoming business looking to take care of our customers, providing a pleasant experience in a warm environment for nourishment that can appeal to any lifestyle.
  • Why should they care? Because the business we are working with will be one that they will want to visit over others in the area.

Competitors

  • Who is the competition (small and large)? Small competition is likely mom n pop diners. Other comfort food establishments. Large competition will be chain style restaurants such as steak houses.
  • What are they telling the audience that WE should also be telling them?: That this experience will be appealing and one that will encourage a return visit
  • What makes us different and unique compared to our competitors? We are different because our product is comfort food with a current and fun twist, we offer something the audience won’t find somewhere else in town.

Tone

  • What tone should use to communicate to our target audience? Why?An airy, eccentric, and friendly one.
  • What adjectives describe the feeling we are trying to project or our approach?
    • Content
    • Amused
    • Fulfilled
    • Intrigued

Message

  • What are we saying with this piece exactly? That this restaurant is a better choice than others. That the customer will easily be able to reach this restaurant and will be welcomed via any mode of communication, whether it be “walk- in”, social media, or through the online ordering platform.
  • Are the words already developed or do we need to develop them? We will develop them.
  • What do we want audiences to take away? That this business is current and savvy and able to interact with the customer in many ways, and is ready to provide a delicious and enjoyable experience in the establishment.
